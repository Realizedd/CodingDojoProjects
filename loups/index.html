  • Assignment Statement Considered Harmfull. Pervasive mutable state make programs more difficult to deal with. While this is old news, most programmers don't know or don't care. It's a pitty, because simple remedies exist.

  • Defining Syntactic Sugar I sometimes hear arguments about what is or is not syntacticc sugar. This is an attempt at defining it

  • Class-based Programming as Syntactic Sugar. Most of the time, "Object Oriented" actually mean classes. Classes are nothing more than a syntactic sugar, at least in statically typed languages such as Java and C++. Sugar is not useless, but seeing past it helps us understand classes better.

  • Taboo "OO" The term "OO" is so overloaded that we should stop using it. Better substitutes are "classes" and "prototypes".
